## Executive summary — the 5 patterns that transfer forward

1. **Ownership Impact (FP #10) is the real constraint in every engagement.** Falcon (9 partners = decision drag), Genesis (Trent 70–80% operational involvement), Hatch (two owners locked in day-to-day), S+A (9 Principals billing $2.6–2.7M each personally). The owner almost never names FP #10 at intake — it surfaces through triangulation. **This is the wedge, validated 4 for 4.**

2. **"Missing middle" is universal at 30–150 staff scale.** Falcon lacks a GM/COO, Genesis has no operating infrastructure under Trent, S+A has no middle management ("Principals are producing, not leading"). The structural gap between owner(s) and doers is what forces FP #10 to show up as a bottleneck — fix the middle layer and the owner problem eases.

3. **Multi-office expansion is a forcing function, not a project.** Genesis (Alberta), S+A (Kelowna/Vancouver/Nanaimo), Falcon (Okanagan multi-office). In every case, expansion exposes pre-existing infrastructure gaps. Sequence structural work first; don't layer geography on top of unresolved friction.

4. **Revenue/margin problems are downstream symptoms.** Both Falcon and Genesis came in framed as "we need to get to $10M" or "we have retention problems." Both engagements reframed those as structure/ownership problems. Pattern: the owner names the symptom; Jirah names the constraint.

5. **Three distinct engagement shapes are in use, not one.** Sprint + Report (Falcon, Genesis) — $50–100k. Monthly retainer — **$10–25k+/month** standard for active advisory / extended-CEO role; Hatch's $1k/mo is a light coaching sub-variant (1 meeting/mo, accountability wrapper only). Parallel strategic + AI tracks (S+A) — $121.5k strategic + AI pilot. Each shape serves a distinct stage / need; stop treating them as one product.

## Per-engagement brief

### Falcon Engineering — Governance unlocks everything else

- **Shape:** 2-day sprint + two-day planning report (~56 pages action register), Q1–Q4 roadmap
- **Client:** Mechanical/Electrical/Energy engineering firm, Okanagan, ~$8.5M rev ($10M target), **9 partners**, ~30–35 staff, 3 technical groups run as separate businesses
- **Primary friction points:** FP #5 Structure (the wedge), FP #1 MVV, FP #2 Revenue, FP #3 Service Mix, FP #4 Strategy, FP #6 Culture, FP #7 Lifecycle, FP #8 Innovation, FP #9 External, FP #10 Ownership Impact
- **Owner framing → surfaced reality:** Came in as a revenue/margin growth problem. Triangulation revealed the real blocker was **9-partner governance creating decision drag, no decision rights, no partner entry/exit/valuation pathway, no GM/COO**. Finding #1 in the report: "Leadership Bandwidth is Maxed."
- **Triangulation evidence:** 32-response staff survey segmented by department (Mech 9.1/10, Elec 8.4/10, Energy 7.0/10), leadership 1-on-1s using the 9 Friction Points as the interview spine, external stakeholder report (GCs / developers / architects). "Hidden Confidence Gap" surfaced: 90% recommend Falcon / only ~60% feel leadership communicates long-term vision.
- **Report structure:** Executive Summary → 9 Friction Points table + 7 Findings That Matter Most → 3 Recommendations × 3 Rocks each (9 rocks total, each with Problem / Success / Why / 0–12 month actions / Partner Commitments / Project Lead Responsibilities / Scorecard / Risks) → Q1–Q4 Roadmap → Partner Workload Summary → 90-Day Quick Wins → 6 Appendices (Leadership Evolution, Partner-Led Limits, Executive Integrator, KPI Benchmarks, KPI Glossary, MVV)
- **Methodology flags:** Scope discipline explicit and baked-in — partners capped at ~5% time, SOPs delegated to Project Lead / PM / Admin-Finance. Jirah framed as "facilitation, tools, system design, training, and support" — not doing the work.
- **Sequencing insight (critical):** "Recommendations 2 & 3 can be implemented in isolation but will exacerbate the friction partners currently experience." → Recommendation 1 (Leadership & Org Foundation) is a hard dependency. Govern first; revenue work follows, or it makes things worse.
- **Transferable insight (1 sentence):** In multi-partner professional-services firms, revenue and margin problems are usually downstream symptoms of an unresolved governance/ownership-structure problem — sequence the structural rock first or the revenue rocks will amplify the friction instead of relieving it.
- **Voice marker:** *"Partners are the fuel for the profitability engine."* Paired with: *"These are not signs of weakness. They are signs of readiness for the next stage."* — names FP #10 without blaming owners.

### Genesis Building Controls — Infrastructure, not revenue

- **Shape:** 2-day strategic sprint + 45-day Strategic & Tactical Plan ($49,900) + Alberta Expansion Preparation Plan ($17,300) → total ~$67k
- **Client:** BMS/controls contractor, founded 2017, ~35 FTE, Kelowna HQ + Surrey/Vancouver (Alberta planned), FY2025 $6.14M (+45% YoY), 68.4% gross margin, 8.3% net margin, owner-operated by Trent Novakowski
- **Primary friction points:** FP #5 Structure (Critical / Very High), FP #7 Lifecycle (Critical / Very High), FP #10 Ownership Impact (Critical / Very High), FP #3 Product/Service Mix (High / Very High — $1M underbuilt service dept at 75% GM)
- **Owner framing → surfaced reality:** Trent framed it as retention + succession + Alberta expansion. Triangulation across 23-person survey + 9 March interviews reframed: **retention risk is a symptom of structural friction**, and the gap to $10M isn't revenue or talent — it's infrastructure and owner-bottleneck. Every interviewee flagged SOP gaps and role ambiguity. Multiple independently named Trent as bottleneck for tool purchasing, hiring, and software decisions.
- **Triangulation evidence:** Interview Validation Summary table explicitly maps each leadership-sprint finding to employee confirmation + Very High/High tags. Methodology made visible inside the deliverable.
- **Report structure:** 65-page PDF with 10-friction-point diagnosis table (Severity/Confidence/Evidence columns), 3-year financial ratio analysis, survey quant + interview validation matrix, workforce growth model by revenue milestone, proposed org chart (named individuals), 6 self-contained Action Plans (~30+ deliverables), Risk Register (13 risks), 24-month execution roadmap (OPS vs BIZ tagged), 90-Day Sprint single-page action register (designed to "print and pin to the wall"), 7 appendices
- **Methodology flags:** Full HR (handbook rewrite, comp modeling) referred out to external partners (Deborah Fox HR, Chris Nielsen RBC) — scope discipline made visible to client. Alberta expansion gated on BC operational readiness via explicit Go/No-Go decision gates.
- **Transferable insight (1 sentence):** For owner-run multi-office services firms, geographic expansion pressure almost always exposes a pre-existing owner-bottleneck + SOP-vacuum that must be named and sequenced first — expansion is the forcing function, not the project.
- **Voice markers:**
  - *"The gap between $6.1M and $10M is not a revenue problem. It is an infrastructure problem."* (executive summary close)
  - Field foreman's *"speed wobble at 25% growth"* — systems break before people do (used as diagnostic headline throughout)
  - Closing: *"The foundation is strong. The market is ready. The team is committed. Now it's time to build the machine."*

### Hatch Interior Design — Retainer as implementation wrapper

- **Shape:** Initial strategic engagement (6 recommendations) → transitioned Oct 1 2025 to month-to-month executive coaching retainer at **$1,000 CAD/month + GST**, 30 days' notice, 1 × 60–90 min in-person meeting/month. Add-on sprints sold separately (Brain Map for DI Launch at **$2,750 fixed**).
- **Client:** Boutique commercial interior design, Kelowna, FY2025 $1.016M sales (up from $873K), normalized EBITDA $354K, net income $270K, small team (Rachel Clarida + Shanna Martin principals; Madison, Amy, Izzy)
- **Primary friction points:** FP #2 Revenue (push to $1.5–2M in 3 yrs, $5M in 10), FP #3 Service Mix (dental vertical spin-off — Dental IDentity), FP #5 Structure (succession equity sale to senior staff, entity structure), FP #7 Lifecycle (acquisition target, commercial property purchase), FP #8 Innovation (DI brand launch), FP #10 Ownership Impact (owner-time valuation, shift owners from daily ops to mentorship)
- **Retainer meeting structure (literal template):** Welcome/Context (10m) → Core Values + 1/3/10-yr targets (5m) → Quarterly Rocks (10–15m) → Issues List (20–25m) → Close & Score (5m)
- **Deliverable shape:** The meeting doc IS the deliverable — living Rocks Tracker + Scorecard Template + Issues List carried month-to-month. Each Rock has owner + due date + status; Issues carry H/M/L priority.
- **Valuation as distinctive offering:** Fall 2025 — $524,812 average across 3 methods (Sales 51% multiplier = $518K; EBITDA 3.5x = $298K; SDE 2.14x = $759K). Purpose: sell equity to senior staff (Madison, Amy). Third-party CBV review (Chad Effa) flagged $500K as likely overvalued given owner-dependence, suggested minority discount, raised profit-sharing-vs-equity as the real question. Joshua reframed the output as **internal negotiation anchor**, not market-clearing price.
- **Methodology flags:** Issues List persists across months (Dec/Jan files near-identical by design) — triangulation sustains through carry-forward. Joshua takes research actions too ("research law firm structures," "research US dental equipment providers") — keeps triangulation alive externally.
- **Transferable insight:** Monthly retainer works as an **implementation accountability wrapper** around a prior strategic engagement — the sprint produces the plan, the retainer keeps the owner honest to it at ~1 hour/month plus ad-hoc add-on sprints for discrete projects.
- **Voice markers:**
  - *"What's the appropriate amount of time to live in the discomfort of change?"* (PM-tool adoption issue framing)
  - Joshua on valuation: *"You're safe with the valuation that your accountant provided… most employees won't have financial knowledge to question."* — pragmatic advisor voice, not academic

### Smith + Andersen — The first parallel-track engagement

- **Shape — Track A (strategic):** $121,500 flat-fee covering succession framework, ops + SOP roadmap, KPI framework, middle-management strategy, 3–5 year growth-plan pressure-test, Gen Z positioning, plus open-ended advisory
- **Shape — Track B (AI pilot):** 4-week, 10 hrs/week (40 hr budget), Apr 2026 start — automated first-pass shop-drawing review for fire-rated assemblies, outputs a findings report with red-bubble markup
- **Client:** Engineering/building systems firm, 140 staff across Kelowna, Vancouver, Nanaimo (grew 45 → 140 in under a decade), 9 Principals billing $2.6–2.7M each, 7% steady annual growth, St. Paul's Hospital marquee project, OG owners hold 50%+ in every office nationally
- **Primary friction points — Track A:** FP #4 Strategy, FP #5 Structure (missing middle + SOPs + operating manager), FP #2 Revenue (utilization/KPI), FP #6 Culture (Gen Z), FP #7 Lifecycle (succession), FP #10 Ownership Impact (the wedge — Principals as top billers)
- **Primary friction points — Track B:** FP #8 Innovation & Change (AI capability build), FP #2 Revenue Generation (leverage on reviewer hours across 200+ sheets/project)
- **The wedge story told to S+A:** "Growth has outpaced your infrastructure. Talent + reputation alone can't carry you to the next stage. Fixing the missing middle is what frees the Principals to actually own the business instead of being its top billers — and without that, the succession story collapses because there's nothing for Associate Principals to succeed into."
- **Mode A visual (Discussion Document):** 11-slide vertical scroll-snap HTML deck, navy #111827 + gold #C5A55A, Playfair Display over DM Sans, nav-dot sidebar, progress bar, slide counter. Arc: Title → Executive Context → Current State (Strengths/Gaps) → The Risk → The JIRAH Growth Way → 9 Friction Points grid → Process → Expertise → Team → Investment → Close. Voice: aphoristic, owner-facing, diagnostic.
- **Mode B visual (Pilot Plan):** Long-scroll editorial in S+A's palette — navy #003167, mustard #FFB500, orange #C26A2B, Montserrat + Proxima Nova Condensed. Hero-meta KV grid (Client / Engagement / Effort / Start / Prepared by). Arc: Problem → Approach → 4-week Timeline → Example Output (fabricated SVG floor plan with 3 red/amber finding bubbles + citation blocks) → Risks → Next Steps. Voice: engineer-to-engineer, numeric, conditional.
- **AI pilot structure (codify for future AI builds):**
  - Fixed 4 weeks, one demo-ready artifact per week ("we avoid the nothing-to-show-until-week-4 trap")
  - W1: Triage + Ingestion; decide Plan A vs B on Day 2 after file inspection
  - W2: Registration (room/grid cross-reference report)
  - W3: Finding Logic (first real findings report + citation plumbing)
  - W4: Markup + Polish (red-bubble PDF output, repeat run, final demo + Phase 2 memo)
  - **Plan A / Plan B fork (explicit):** A = vector PDF pipeline (pdfplumber + PyMuPDF + Claude Sonnet on ambiguous matches; ~30 sec/sheet, ~$0.02/sheet); B = vision-model fallback (Claude vision + Tesseract OCR; ~2 min/sheet, ~$0.25/sheet). Decision Day 2.
  - **0-hallucination commitment:** Stated twice — as stat ("0 · Hallucinated findings — every call cites source doc + sheet + grid") and as hard constraint ("Zero-hallucination is a hard constraint, not a nice-to-have")
  - **3 paired risk cards:** Drawing registration fails → "W2 dedicated to this alone"; Scanned-only drawings → "narrow scope to 1 project"; False positives erode trust → "high confidence thresholds — 'no finding' is always a valid answer"
- **Transferable insights:**
  1. *Strategic track:* Engineering firms past ~100 staff hit the wall at the same place — Principals still billing individually at $2M+/head with no middle layer. The sellable wedge is framing "missing middle" as a **succession blocker**, not an HR problem (no middle = nothing for next-gen to succeed into → retention and equity pathway both collapse).
  2. *AI pilot track:* First-time AI buyer needs the pilot to feel like a **known-quantity engineering contract**, not a research project. Fix week count, price the hours, commit to a weekly demo artifact, pre-commit Plan B for the most likely technical failure, and show the final deliverable as a mock-up on Day 0 so the client reacts to a picture instead of a promise.
- **Voice markers:**
  - Mode A: *"Growth Without Structure Is a Ceiling, Not a Strategy."*
  - Mode A: *"Strong technical firms with solid reputations hit a ceiling when operational maturity doesn't match their scale. That's exactly where we operate."*
  - Mode A: *"Principals are producing, not leading — no capacity for strategy or succession."*
  - Mode B: *"We won't know which approach is right until we see S+A's actual drawing files. Plan A assumes vector PDFs. Plan B assumes scanned raster. Day 1 is triage on real files."*
  - Mode B: *"A reviewer who gets 20 bogus flags on the first sheet stops reading. Zero-hallucination is a hard constraint, not a nice-to-have."*
- **Voice contrast:** Mode A is declarative and diagnostic ("is a ceiling, not a strategy"); Mode B is conditional and empirical ("we won't know until we see the files"). Same firm, same Jirah — different register for different buyer.

## Cross-engagement patterns (the synthesis)

### Pattern 1 — FP #10 Ownership Impact is the terminal diagnosis in every engagement

| Engagement | How FP #10 shows up |
|---|---|
| Falcon | 9 partners billing at $2M+ — leadership bandwidth maxed, no GM/COO |
| Genesis | Trent 70–80% operational, named as bottleneck for purchasing/hiring/software decisions |
| Hatch | 2 owner-principals need to shift from daily ops to strategy/mentorship |
| S+A | 9 Principals billing $2.6–2.7M personally — "producing, not leading" |

**Implication:** The Friction Audit can assume FP #10 will be the real constraint before day 1 starts. Shift Jirah's signature POV to: *"In owner-run B2B firms, the real constraint is almost always what the owner is personally holding on to."* Less hedged than current phrasing, now supported by 4/4.

### Pattern 2 — The "missing middle" is the structural signature

30–150 staff firms share the same structural gap: owners at the top, doers at the bottom, nothing in between. At Jirah's ICP scale, **no GM/COO or equivalent mid-management layer** is the single most common structural finding.

**Implication:** The audit has a high-prior finding before it starts. Probe: who owns decisions under $X threshold? When the owner is on vacation for 2 weeks, what breaks first?

### Pattern 3 — Expansion exposes, it doesn't cause

Multi-office / multi-province expansion shows up in 3 of 4 engagements. In every case, expansion pressure surfaces pre-existing operational gaps — SOP vacuum, decision-rights ambiguity, culture-transmission problems.

**Implication:** Discovery should ask: "When do you plan to expand?" If the answer is "soon," the engagement scope should include a sequencing framework (Genesis-style Go/No-Go gates) not just the expansion plan itself.

### Pattern 4 — Owners name symptoms; Jirah names constraints

Owner intake framing vs. Jirah's diagnostic finding:

| Client | Owner said | Jirah found |
|---|---|---|
| Falcon | "Get to $10M, improve margin" | "9-partner governance is the ceiling" |
| Genesis | "Retention, succession, Alberta" | "Infrastructure problem, not revenue problem" |
| S+A | "Gen Z retention, succession risk" | "No middle layer means nowhere to succeed into" |
| Hatch | "Spin off Dental IDentity" | "Shift owners from daily ops to mentorship" |

**Implication:** The Discussion Document should be structured as **two columns**: *What you told us* | *What we think the real question is.* This is currently implicit — make it explicit.

### Pattern 5 — Three engagement shapes, not one

| Shape | Duration | Price anchor | Use case | Exemplar |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sprint + Report** | 2 days sprint + 45-day report | $50–100k | First strategic engagement, diagnostic-to-playbook | Falcon ($75–100k+), Genesis ($49.9k + $17.3k = $67.2k) |
| **Active Advisory Retainer** | Ongoing monthly, 30-day notice | **$10–25k+/month** | Extended-CEO role post-sprint, multiple touchpoints/week, active implementation partner | Standard Jirah retainer shape |
| **Light Coaching Retainer** (sub-variant) | Month-to-month, 30-day notice | ~$1k/mo + add-on sprints at $2.75k fixed | Lightweight accountability wrapper (1 meeting/mo only), hands-off | Hatch — outlier rate |
| **Parallel Strategic + AI** | $121.5k strategic + separately scoped AI pilot | S+A track-A = $121.5k; AI pilot = 4-week hourly | Firm where AI leverage is a visible co-equal opportunity alongside strategic needs | S+A |

**Implication:** Sales funnel should route prospects to the right shape. Friction Audit ($1.5k / 1 day) is the front door to any of these three — it diagnoses which shape fits.

### Pattern 6 — Triangulation is visible inside the deliverable, not just performed

Falcon and Genesis both make triangulation **visible to the client** — the Interview Validation Matrix at Genesis, the survey-by-department scoring at Falcon, the External Stakeholder Report at Falcon with the "Confidential — Partner Review Draft" label. Clients see the methodology; it's not backstage work.

**Implication:** Every action register should have a "How we arrived here" section naming the triangulation evidence per finding. Sells the methodology each engagement.

### Pattern 7 — Scope discipline gets written into the deliverable

Falcon's report explicitly caps partner time at ~5% and delegates SOP authoring to Project Lead / PM / Admin-Finance. Genesis refers HR work to external partners by name (Deborah Fox HR, Chris Nielsen RBC). Clients see the discipline; scope creep dies in the report structure, not in side conversations.

**Implication:** Action register template should include a "Not in this register — referred elsewhere" section.

### Pattern 8 — Reframe FP #10 as growth-stage readiness, never as weakness

Falcon's money line: *"These are not signs of weakness. They are signs of readiness for the next stage."* This is how FP #10 gets diagnosed without the owner getting defensive. Consistent across engagements.

**Implication:** Friction Audit skill should default-include this reframe when surfacing FP #10 findings.
