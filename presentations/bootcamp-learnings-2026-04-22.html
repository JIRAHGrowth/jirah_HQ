What This Memo Is

Source, frame, and how to read it.

Joshua attended Session 1 (original + re-run) of The Effortless Business Bootcamp — Callan Faulkner, founder of The Uncommon Business. The session walked through how she runs a reported $16M creator-education company on ~25 humans + ~150 "AI employees," demonstrating a stack of Claude Projects, Skills, and scheduled agents covering marketing, sales, customer success, and executive ops.

This is not a client analysis and not methodology theory. It is a partner-level decision doc: what affirms the JIRAH build, what gaps it reveals, what architecture shifts it proposes, and — most importantly — a prioritized list of what to act on, with rationale.

Deeper Rethinks

Four architecture shifts worth a partner decision.

These are not tweaks. These change how we package and price AI work — and how we talk about it on a pitch call. Each has an opportunity cost; we should decide before we act.

Shift 1 — Language
"AI Pilot" → "AI Employee."

NOW: We sell AI Pilots. One-time builds, fixed-fee.

"Pilot" = the build project. "AI Employee" = the deployed artifact. Keeps the pilot nomenclature but names what the client is actually buying. Sharper pricing story, cleaner case-study copy.

Shift 2 — Delivery
Ship a Client Business Brain with every pilot.

NOW: We ship an HTML pilot plan + a working tool. Client owns both artifacts; nothing compounds.

Ship a Claude Project populated with their SOPs, policies, financials, prior case studies, compliance context. Every future pilot builds on top of it. Turns one-off builds into a per-client asset base.

Shift 3 — Role
Name "AI Architect" as a JIRAH retainer lane.

NOW: "Extended CEO" is the positioning. AI work sits inside it but isn't named as a role the client can buy.

Package "AI Architect in residence" as a distinct Step 6 retainer lane — parallel to ops advisory, not sub-ordinate. Lets us sell AI capacity to clients who don't yet have a strategic AI problem but want the in-house capability growing.

Shift 4 — Pricing
Per-AI-employee monthly ($6–10K/mo) as a new tier.

NOW: Fixed-fee pilot (~$18K anchor S+A) → monthly retainer. No recurring AI-specific SKU.

Introduce a recurring per-employee tier between pilot and retainer. $6–10K/mo per deployed AI employee, bundled with monthly tune-and-tweak + compliance review. Solves the "we built it, now what?" moment every pilot hits.

Do Not Copy

Where her playbook breaks on our ICP.

This is not her failure — it is a voice and audience mismatch. Her ICP (female founders, coaches, creators, $500K–$100M) is not ours. Lifting her tactics wholesale would cheapen JIRAH's authority positioning with a 30-year-veteran engineering-firm owner.

The voice and vibe.

"Buckle up buttercups," "boss babes," emoji-heavy, all-caps YES, cortisol language. Direct opposite of Mode A partner voice. Do not copy verbatim quotes into client artifacts — reframe through JIRAH voice rules first.

Unverified outcome claims.

"$16M revenue," "~50% margins," "150 AI employees." Treat as marketing copy. When citing in JIRAH content, use "reported" hedging and tie arguments to the architecture patterns, not the numbers.

"AI Revenue Coach" as lead magnet.

Works for a $97-ticket consumer funnel. Would signal wrong altitude to a $6–20M owner evaluating whether to trust us with a partner seat. Do not clone.

Always-on spare laptop in the office.

Fine for a $16M creator shop. Wrong advice for an engineering firm with IT governance. When our pilot plans ship scheduled-task logic, route it through cloud-scheduler infrastructure — never through a lone workstation.

"Keep all your existing tools" as a philosophy.

Oversimplifies. JIRAH clients often do need tool rationalization; blanket "just add Claude" contradicts our scope-discipline principle. Keep the nuance on a case-by-case basis.

"End of human mediocrity" middle-manager rhetoric.

Punches down at mid-level employees. Conflicts with JIRAH's respect-for-the-existing-team tone. We sell to owners, but we triangulate with their teams — those teams hear our posture. Avoid.

Upload-everything-to-Claude posture.

Her own HIPAA carve-out shows the line is real. Accounting SIN/SSN, engineering PE-stamped drawings, privileged legal doc — all need per-client data-handling protocols. Do not import the "comfortable putting financial data in" posture without the carve-out discipline.

"Nvidia engineers spend their salary on tokens."

Useful anchor, but it's a chip-design context. Don't present as a normative benchmark in owner-run B2B services proposals — the number won't translate and will discredit the rest of the argument.
