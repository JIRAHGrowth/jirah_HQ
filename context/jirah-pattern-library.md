## Cross-engagement patterns (validated 4/4)

### 1. FP #10 Ownership Impact is the terminal diagnosis
Every engagement (Falcon, Genesis, Hatch, S+A) surfaces owner/partner-as-bottleneck as the real constraint, even when the owner frames the intake problem as revenue, retention, or expansion. **Assume FP #10 will be the finding before day 1 starts.** Probe: when the owner is on vacation 2 weeks, what breaks? What decisions still route to them that shouldn't?

### 2. "Missing middle" is the structural signature at 30–150 staff
No GM/COO, no formalized middle-management layer, owners/partners still billing personally. Falcon (9 partners, no GM), S+A (9 Principals billing $2.6–2.7M each, no middle), Genesis (no ops infrastructure under Trent). At this scale it's the default state, not the exception.

### 3. Multi-office expansion exposes pre-existing friction
Genesis (Alberta), S+A (Kelowna/Vancouver/Nanaimo), Falcon (Okanagan multi-office). Expansion pressure surfaces SOP vacuum, decision-rights ambiguity, culture-transmission gaps. Discovery question: *"When do you plan to expand?"* — if the answer is "soon," scope a sequencing framework with Go/No-Go gates (Genesis pattern).

### 4. Owners name symptoms; Jirah names constraints
| Client | Owner framing | Jirah finding |
|---|---|---|
| Falcon | "Get to $10M" | "9-partner governance is the ceiling" |
| Genesis | "Retention + Alberta" | "Infrastructure problem, not revenue problem" |
| S+A | "Gen Z retention" | "No middle layer = nowhere to succeed into" |
| Hatch | "Spin off Dental IDentity" | "Shift owners from daily ops to mentorship" |

Structure Discussion Documents with two columns: *What you told us* | *What we think the real question is*.

### 5. Three engagement shapes — route prospects to the right one
| Shape | Price | Duration | Exemplar |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sprint + Report** | $50–100k | 2-day sprint + 45-day report | Falcon, Genesis ($49.9k + $17.3k) |
| **Active Advisory Retainer** | **$10–25k+/month** | Ongoing M2M, 30-day notice; extended-CEO role | Standard Jirah retainer |
| **Light Coaching Retainer** (sub-variant) | ~$1k/mo + $2.75k/add-on sprint | M2M, 30-day notice, 1 meeting/mo only | Hatch (outlier rate) |
| **Parallel Strategic + AI** | $121.5k + AI hourly | 6-month strategic + 4-week pilot | S+A |

Friction Audit ($1.5k / 1 day) is front door; it diagnoses which shape fits. Most post-sprint retainers should land in the Active Advisory range, not the light-coaching rate.

### 6. Triangulation is visible inside the deliverable, not backstage
Falcon: Interview Validation Matrix, department-level survey scoring, External Stakeholder Report. Genesis: Interview Validation Summary mapping leadership-sprint findings to employee confirmation. The methodology is literally shown to the client; it sells itself each engagement.

### 7. Scope discipline gets written into the report structure
Falcon caps partner time at ~5% inside the deliverable. Genesis names external referral partners (Deborah Fox HR, Chris Nielsen RBC) in the report. Scope creep dies on the page, not in side conversations. Action Register template should include: *"Not in this register — referred elsewhere."*

### 8. FP #10 is reframed as growth-stage readiness
The diplomatic diagnosis: *"These are not signs of weakness. They are signs of readiness for the next stage."* (Falcon) — names owner-bottleneck without making owners defensive. Use consistently across engagements when FP #10 is the finding.

## Engagement history (primary sources — lineage)

### Vulcan — Culture / Vision (pre-OneDrive, unverified in this session)
Referenced as first engagement in the lineage. Primary files not in current archive. Known pattern: values vacuum in technical firm surfaced through exit-interview signals → MVV codification unlocks hiring consistency.

### Hatch Interior Design — Light coaching retainer exemplar
Initial strategic engagement → transitioned Oct 2025 to **light coaching retainer** ($1k CAD/mo + GST, M2M, 30-day notice, 1 × 60–90 min in-person meeting/mo). Add-on sprints at $2,750 fixed (e.g., Brain Map for DI Launch). Hatch's rate is an **outlier at the low end** — standard active-advisory Jirah retainers are $10–25k+/mo. Hatch is a hands-off accountability wrapper, not a full extended-CEO engagement. Meeting template: Welcome → Core Values / 1-3-10 yr → Quarterly Rocks → Issues List → Close & Score. Rocks Tracker + Scorecard + Issues List carried month-to-month.
Distinctive offering: business valuation work ($524k avg across 3 methods — Sales mult, EBITDA, SDE). Framed as internal negotiation anchor, not market-clearing price.
**Transferable:** The *light-coaching* shape exists as a distinct product tier — use it for post-sprint firms that want accountability only, not active advisory. Default most post-sprint engagements into the standard Active Advisory Retainer tier ($10–25k+/mo), not Hatch's rate.

### Falcon Engineering — Governance-first sequencing
2-day sprint + 56-page action register + Q1–Q4 roadmap. Mech/Elec/Energy consulting firm, Okanagan, ~$8.5M rev, 9 partners, ~30–35 staff. **Core wedge: 9-partner governance creating decision drag.** Recommendation 1 (Leadership & Org Foundation) is a hard dependency before Recs 2 & 3 (revenue + service mix) — otherwise those recs *exacerbate* the friction. Triangulation: 32-response staff survey segmented by department, leadership 1-on-1s indexed to 9 Friction Points, external stakeholder report.
**Transferable:** In multi-partner pro services firms, revenue/margin problems are downstream symptoms of an unresolved governance problem. Sequence the structural rock first.

### Genesis Building Controls — Infrastructure-not-revenue reframe
2-day sprint ($49.9k) + 65-page Strategic Planning Report + Alberta Expansion Preparation Plan ($17.3k). BMS/controls contractor, ~35 FTE, Kelowna HQ + Surrey/Vancouver, $6.14M (+45% YoY), 68.4% GM, 8.3% NM. **Core wedge: retention and expansion pressure are symptoms of infrastructure + owner-bottleneck.** Trent at 70–80% operational involvement. Triangulation: 23-person survey + 9 March interviews + Interview Validation Summary. Deliverable includes 10-friction-point diagnosis table, financial ratio analysis, workforce growth model, proposed org chart with named individuals, 6 Action Plans (~30+ deliverables), 13-risk Risk Register, 24-month roadmap (OPS vs BIZ tagged), 90-Day Sprint single-page action register designed to "print and pin to the wall."
Scope discipline visible: HR referred out to Deborah Fox / Chris Nielsen. Alberta gated on BC readiness via Go/No-Go decision gates.
**Transferable:** For owner-run multi-office firms, geographic expansion exposes owner-bottleneck + SOP vacuum. Expansion is the forcing function, not the project.

### Smith + Andersen — First parallel-track engagement (strategic + AI)
- **Track A (strategic, $121.5k flat):** succession framework, ops + SOP roadmap, KPI framework, middle-management strategy, 3–5 year growth pressure-test, Gen Z positioning, advisory tier.
- **Track B (AI pilot, 40-hr budget over 4 weeks):** automated first-pass shop-drawing review for fire-rated assemblies, red-bubble markup output.

Engineering/building systems firm, 140 staff across Kelowna/Vancouver/Nanaimo, grew 45→140 in under a decade, 9 Principals billing $2.6–2.7M each. Core wedge: "missing middle" framed as **succession blocker**, not an HR problem.

**AI pilot structure — now canonical for future AI builds:**
- Fixed 4 weeks, weekly demo-able artifact, "we avoid the 'nothing to show until week 4' trap"
- **Plan A / Plan B fork** decided Day 2 after real-file triage (A = vector PDF pipeline; B = vision-model fallback)
- **0-hallucination commitment** stated as stat + hard constraint, with citation schema (source doc + sheet + grid)
- **3 paired risk cards** — each risk gets a pre-committed mitigation
- **Day-0 mock-up deliverable:** fabricated SVG example output so client reacts to a picture, not a promise

**Transferable (2):**
1. *Strategic track:* Engineering firms past ~100 staff hit the same wall — Principals billing individually, no middle layer. Pitch "missing middle" as succession blocker, not HR.
2. *AI track:* First-time AI buyer needs the pilot to feel like a **known-quantity engineering contract**, not research. Fix weeks, price hours, weekly demo artifact, pre-commit Plan B, show the final deliverable as a mock-up on Day 0.
