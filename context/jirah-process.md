# Jirah's 6-Step Engagement Process

Every strategic-consulting engagement follows this flow. AI-build engagements have a parallel 4-week pilot track (see [jirah-pattern-library.md](jirah-pattern-library.md) and the Smith + Andersen Shop Drawing Pilot exemplar).

---

## Step 1 — Initial Discovery

**Purpose:** Meet the owner, understand the presenting challenge, and assess fit against ICP.

**Deliverable:** Discovery call notes (internal). Fit decision made.

**Cadence:** One 60-minute call. Joshua leads, Jason observes.

**Exit criteria:** ICP fit confirmed. Owner willing to share data and make team available. Both partners agree to proceed.

---

## Step 2 — Discussion Document

**Purpose:** Codify the objectives, scope, and approach for the engagement. This becomes the shared contract for what we will and won't do.

**Deliverable:** Discussion Document (Mode A — JIRAH-branded). Exemplars: `C:\Users\jason\Downloads\Discussion Document - S&A.pdf`, `SA_Discussion_Document.html`.

**Structure:** What we heard → what we think matters → scope of the sprint → out-of-scope (explicitly named — SOPs, HR policy, handbooks usually) → timeline → investment.

**Cadence:** Drafted within 5 business days of Discovery. Owner reviews; we revise once.

---

## Step 3 — Kickoff Meeting

**Purpose:** Align the client team on the sprint, set expectations, secure time/data commitments.

**Deliverable:** Kickoff agenda + post-kickoff internal prep notes.

**Cadence:** 60–90 minutes. Typically triggers the sprint within 1–2 weeks.

---

## Step 4 — Strategic Sprint (1–3 days)

**Purpose:** Owner-led deep dive across the 10 Friction Points. Intense, concentrated, immersive.

**Cadence:** 1 to 3 consecutive days. Joshua facilitates; Jason analyzes and takes notes in real time. All sessions transcribed via Fireflies.ai.

**Exit outputs (internal):** Sprint field notes, session transcripts, preliminary hypotheses keyed to friction points, list of team interviews and external checks required for triangulation.

---

## Step 5 — Report & Plan

**Purpose:** Deliver the playbook. This is an **action register, not a narrative report.**

**Deliverable shape:**
- Executive summary — the real constraint (our triangulated finding), confidence level
- Findings mapped to the 10 Friction Points, each with confidence tag
- **Prioritized action register** — each item has: priority (Critical/High/Medium), owner (client-side), friction point mapped, concrete next step, deadline, dependencies
- Risks with mitigations
- Out-of-scope reminder — what we did NOT address (so scope creep is named, not drifted into)
- Next steps for implementation / KPI tracking phase

**Visual mode:** A (JIRAH-branded) for the strategic layer; action register may export to xlsx for the client's PM tool.

**Cadence:** Delivered 30–45 days after sprint end (Genesis was 45-day). Includes employee interviews (typically 6–10 stakeholders) and external checks before finalization.

---

## Step 6 — KPI Tracking

**Purpose:** Ongoing implementation support. This is the retainer revenue tail.

**Deliverable:** Monthly or quarterly KPI dashboard + action-register status update.

**Cadence:** Monthly check-ins usually; can flex to quarterly for lighter retainers.

**Partner time:** ~5% owner time on implementation — work flows through PMs and JIRAH, not the owner.

---

## Tailored engagement design — not one-size-fits-all

Each engagement's scope is shaped to the friction pattern, not a fixed template:
- **Vulcan** — culture/vision work
- **Hatch** — broad strategic growth, 6 recommendations
- **Falcon** — governance + cross-group ops, 12-month Q1–Q4 roadmap
- **Genesis** — tactical SOPs, BMS vertical

See [jirah-pattern-library.md](jirah-pattern-library.md) for the lineage and transferrable patterns.
