# Jirah Methodology — Discipline Markers

The 10 Friction Points are the WHAT. These are the HOW. All of them are non-negotiable.

---

## 1. Triangulation

**Rule:** Owner hypothesis must be cross-checked against team interviews AND external stakeholders before a recommendation lands.

**Why:** Owners often misdiagnose their own business. The Ownership Impact lens (#10) is precisely the reason we can't trust the owner's self-report as the only input.

**Confidence tagging (on every finding):**
- **Very High** — owner + 3+ team interviews + external signal / data
- **High** — owner + 2+ team interviews OR owner + strong data signal
- **Medium** — owner hypothesis + partial validation; flagged for further probing

**Example:** Genesis engagement — Trent's sprint findings validated against 8 employee interviews before inclusion in the final report. Findings that didn't survive triangulation were downgraded or dropped.

---

## 2. Dual-Facilitator Model

**Rule:** Joshua leads facilitation. Jason analyzes, takes notes, watches for language shifts / contradiction / emotional markers.

**Why:** One person cannot facilitate + observe + take notes simultaneously. The observer catches what the facilitator, who is busy running the room, cannot.

**Protocol:** Every session recorded via Fireflies.ai. Jason's real-time notes focus on *pattern signals* (change in tone when a topic comes up, contradictions between stated and revealed, who talks vs who goes silent), not literal transcription — the transcript covers that.

---

## 3. Scope Discipline

**Explicitly OUT of scope** on most engagements (flag early to prevent creep):
- SOP writing
- Employee handbooks
- Full HR policy drafting

**Why:** These are execution artifacts, not strategic-growth work. They expand scope without expanding value, and they belong to the client's operations team or an HR-specialist vendor.

**Exceptions:** Tactical SOP work can be explicitly scoped in (Genesis engagement), but only when named in the Discussion Document up front.

---

## 4. Action Registers, NOT Reports

**Rule:** Every Step-5 deliverable is a prioritized action register, not a narrative report.

**Register item format:**
- Priority: Critical / High / Medium
- Friction point mapped (1–10)
- Finding (one sentence) + confidence tag
- Recommendation (one sentence)
- Owner (client-side person)
- Concrete next step (verb-led)
- Deadline
- Dependencies

**Why:** Narrative reports go on the shelf. Action registers get implemented. The register is the working playbook — it lives in the client's PM tool after we hand off.

---

## 5. ~5% Owner Time on Implementation

**Rule:** During the KPI Tracking phase (Step 6), the owner's time commitment is ~5%. Work flows through PMs and JIRAH, not the owner.

**Why:** The Ownership Impact problem (#10) often IS the bottleneck. If our execution phase routes through the owner, we're re-creating the problem we just diagnosed.

**How:** Assign work to operational leads in the client org. JIRAH acts as the extended CEO team, unblocking and reviewing, not funneling everything through the owner.

---

## 6. Pattern Library

**Rule:** Each engagement informs the next. Transfer patterns forward.

**Lineage:** Vulcan → Hatch → Falcon → Genesis (→ current engagements). See [jirah-pattern-library.md](jirah-pattern-library.md) for details.

**Why:** Consulting firms live or die on repeatable insight. Boutique consultancies that don't compound knowledge plateau at founder-capacity.

**How:** After each engagement closes, harvest 2–3 transferable patterns (what friction signal appeared, what diagnostic worked, what recommendation moved the needle) into the library for reuse.

---

## Tool Stack

- **Fireflies.ai** — transcription on ALL sessions (sprint, interviews, kickoffs)
- **ChatGPT / Claude** — synthesis, report drafting, language-pattern analysis of transcripts
- **Dual human facilitators** — keep AI honest; AI is a synthesis engine, not a judgment engine

AI supports, it does not decide. Every recommendation is human-reviewed and -owned.
