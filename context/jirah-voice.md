# Jirah Voice and Reusable Deliverable Structure

Applies to every Jirah-authored artifact: emails, proposals, pilot plans, pitch decks, LinkedIn posts, audit findings, reports, landing pages.

---

## Voice Markers

**1. Direct, factual, no marketing fluff.**
Example: *"Reviewers spend hours cross-checking drawings for the same rated-assembly issues."*
Anti-pattern: *"In today's fast-paced world, efficiency is more important than ever."*

**2. Embed proofs of discipline in the prose.**
Examples:
- *"0 hallucinated findings — every call cites source doc + sheet + grid."*
- *"We avoid the 'nothing to show until week 4' trap."*
- *"Every recommendation is triangulated across owner + team + external before it ships."*

**3. Plan A / Plan B decision forks** when there's uncertainty.
Pattern: *"Plan A: [preferred path]. Plan B: [fallback]. Day 2 decision trigger: [observable signal in real data]."*
Never hedge — name both paths AND the trigger condition. We commit to deciding, not to waffling.

**4. Risks with mitigations.**
Always transparent about what could go wrong, always paired with how we handle it. Section can be titled "What could go wrong and how we handle it" — direct and plain-spoken.

**5. Weekly deliverables.**
Every week ends with something demo-able. No "nothing to show until week 4" patterns. Build the demo cadence into the proposal.

**6. Commit to numbers, not adjectives.**
When in doubt, cut an adjective and add a number. *"Reduces review time"* → *"Cuts drawing-review time from 4 hours to 40 minutes per submittal."*

---

## Reusable Deliverable Structure

For proposals, pilot plans, pitches, landing pages:

```
Hero
  ↓
Problem (with stat stack — concrete, measured)
  ↓
Approach (Plan A preferred / Plan B fallback, decision trigger)
  ↓
Timeline (weekly deliverables — each week ends with something demo-able)
  ↓
Example Output (fabricated or real — make it tangible)
  ↓
Risks + Mitigations
  ↓
Next Steps (usually two 1-hour kickoff meetings)
```

## Naming Conventions

- **Formal:** JIRAH Growth Partners (proposals, contracts, branded docs)
- **Body copy:** Jirah (emails, LinkedIn, casual prose)
- **Never:** "The JIRAH" / "a JIRAH consultant" / generic corporate treatment

---

## Email Sign-offs

Joint emails from Jason + Joshua: sign **"Jason and Joshua."** Single-author emails: sign with first name only.

---

## Don'ts

- No corporate-speak ("leverage synergies," "paradigm shift," "best-in-class")
- No em-dashes in drafts unless they serve a real purpose
- No generic industry flattery ("Your industry is evolving rapidly...")
- No hedging on recommendations — if confidence is Medium, say Medium; don't soft-pedal
- No unbacked claims — every strong statement needs a triangulated source or a measurable commitment
