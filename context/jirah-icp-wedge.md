# JIRAH ICP, Wedge, and Friction Audit Funnel

## Ideal Client Profile

**Size:** 30–150 employees, $6M–$20M ARR, 6-figure contracts (per engagement).

**Core vertical:** Engineering and building systems firms — multi-office, multi-principal, scaling past 100 people. Also: professional services, accounting, similar B2B owner-run firms. **AI-build clients can go bigger** (no revenue cap).

**Buyer:** Owner or managing partner.

**Pattern we recognize:** Strong technical firm, solid reputation, steady growth, hits a ceiling when operational maturity lags scale. That is where Jirah operates.

## The Wedge (POV)

*"In owner-run B2B firms, the real constraint is almost never what the owner thinks it is. We find it. Then we execute — often with custom AI — to remove it."*

The Ownership Impact lens (Friction Point #10) is the specific wedge most consultants will not name. Lean into it.

## Three Monetization Paths (off one diagnostic entry)

1. **Ops optimization** — remove the constraint the audit surfaces
2. **High-value project execution** — new location, new service line, M&A integration, succession framework, etc.
3. **Custom AI build** — bespoke AI tool integrated into client workflow; higher contract value, strongest differentiator

## Two Funnels — Route by Geography

Jirah runs two parallel front doors. Pick the right one per prospect. **Jason and Joshua are closers in person.** The whole system is designed to get qualified prospects across a table from them as early as possible when geography allows.

### Funnel 1 — Local (Kelowna / Okanagan) → Lunch Front Door

```
Warm intro, industry-pulse trigger, or inbound
                  ↓
        15-min phone screen (ICP fit check)
                  ↓
        Lunch with Jason + Joshua
                  ↓
        Paid Friction Audit — $1,500
        1 full day on-site
        Few-page findings (action register)
                  ↓
        Expansion conversation:
        ops / project / AI engagement
```

**Use when:** prospect is within driving distance of Kelowna.

**Why lunch works here:** owner-run B2B firms in smaller markets buy from people they've sat across a table from. In-person is the wedge that generic consultancies cannot replicate at scale. Lunch replaces the application form because Jason and Joshua can read fit in real time.

**Lunch discipline:**
- Not open-ended coffee. Warm-intro, trigger-hit, or phone-screened only.
- Soft audit pitch baked in: *"If there's a fit, the way we usually start is a 1-day audit — $1,500, here's what you get, here's what happens next."*
- Post-lunch debrief logged to prospect's OneDrive `.md` with next action + deadline.

### Funnel 2 — Out-of-Market → Application Front Door

```
Free "Apply for Audit" form  →  JIRAH handpicks strongest ICP fits
                                          ↓
                              Paid Friction Audit — $1,500
                              1 full day on-site or virtual
                              Few-page findings (action register)
                                          ↓
                              Expansion conversation:
                              ops / project / AI engagement
```

**Use when:** prospect is outside driving distance. Volume lane. Runs alongside `/jirah-lead-gen` and `/jirah-industry-pulse` mass outreach.

**Why this shape:**
- **Volume at top** (free apply form, surfaces demand + lead-source data)
- **Prestige in middle** (selective — not everyone qualifies)
- **Commitment test** ($1.5k paid — owners who won't pay for diagnostic won't pay for execution)
- **Constrained delivery** (1 day + few pages — repeatable, scalable, aligned with action-register-not-report philosophy)

**Apply-form design principle:** questions must surface the 10 Friction Points signals (e.g., "What do you think is holding you back?" answered against what the data suggests once inside the audit).

**Declined applicants:** soft offer of a free 15-min strategic conversation (referral/content path). Deferred until Sprint 2.

## Funnel Routing Rule

When drafting outreach, triage, or sequencing for a specific prospect, Claude must first check geography:
- **Within ~4-hour drive of Kelowna → Funnel 1 (Lunch).** Draft a lunch ask, not an audit-application link.
- **Outside that radius → Funnel 2 (Application).** Drive them to the audit apply form.

Both funnels converge on the same paid Friction Audit as the first contracted engagement. The difference is how we earn the right to invoice.
