# The 10 Friction Points — Jirah's Diagnostic IP

Every engagement is diagnosed through these 10 lenses. Every audit finding, action-register item, and proposal must map to one (or more). Points 1–9 are standard strategic-growth lenses. **#10 Ownership Impact is unique to Jirah** and is the wedge most consultants won't name.

---

## 1. Mission / Vision / Values

Guiding purpose, principles, and behavioral norms.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Is the mission codified and known by staff beyond leadership?
- Do hiring, firing, and capital-allocation decisions reference it?
- When values conflict with a profitable action, which wins? (Tells you if values are real.)

## 2. Revenue Generation

Lifeblood of growth and sustainability — pipeline, sales motion, pricing, concentration risk.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Client concentration — top 3 clients = what % of revenue?
- Win rate by stage; cycle length trending which direction?
- Pricing discipline — when did we last raise fees? Are we leaving money on the table?
- Who owns business development? Is it the partners, or is it systemic?

## 3. Product and Service Mix

Balance of offerings that create value; portfolio-level economics.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Margin by service line — are we subsidizing loss-leaders?
- Which offerings are growing / flat / shrinking, and why?
- Any services that are commoditizing and need to be moved up-market or killed?

## 4. Strategy

Roadmap connecting vision to execution — 3/5-year plan, resource allocation, review cadence.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Is there a written strategy? When was it last reviewed?
- Are capital / headcount / time allocated in line with the strategy, or drifting?
- What's the one bet that, if it works, changes the firm? Who owns it?

## 5. Company Structure

Framework enabling scale and decision-making — org chart, span of control, decision rights.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Is there a "missing middle" — work bottlenecking because there's no operational layer between partners and doers?
- Decision rights clear — who approves what, at what threshold, without escalating?
- Span of control reasonable? (>8 direct reports is usually a structural smell.)

## 6. Company Culture

DNA shaping behavior, attracting talent, retention, psychological safety.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Voluntary regrettable-turnover rate — which segments?
- What do exit interviews say the firm is actually like, not what leadership says it is?
- Gen Z retention specifically — are we reading their expectations or bolting them onto boomer/GenX norms?

## 7. Lifecycle

Current growth stage and operational capacity match — startup / scale-up / mature / pivot.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Does the operating rhythm (meetings, reviews, cadence) match current scale, or is it startup-era?
- Are systems (financial, PM, HR) sized for today or three years ago?
- Capacity — where are we bumping ceilings (people, systems, capital, leadership bandwidth)?

## 8. Innovation & Change

Adaptability to evolve with industry and technology — R&D rhythm, tool adoption, change fatigue.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- How has the firm adopted AI and new tools in the last 18 months? Leading, following, or frozen?
- Where could AI create 10x leverage? (Always scan this — feeds the AI-build path.)
- Change fatigue — has leadership over-announced too many initiatives without finishing any?

## 9. External Environment

Market, regulatory, cultural, competitive forces acting on the firm.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- What's the 3-year outlook for our core market? Tailwind, headwind, flat?
- Regulatory shifts on the horizon that change our cost structure or scope?
- Competitive landscape — who's compressing our margins, who's moving upmarket, who's disrupting?

## 10. Ownership Impact — The Jirah Wedge

Owner-as-bottleneck. The constraint that most consultants won't name because the owner is the buyer.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- What happens at the firm when the owner is on vacation for 2 weeks?
- What decisions still route through the owner that shouldn't?
- Has the owner named a successor? A timeline? A mechanism? Or is it "someday"?
- Does the firm's growth ceiling match the owner's personal capacity ceiling? If yes, that's the constraint.

This is often the real answer when an owner thinks the problem is "my ops" or "my people." Surface it diplomatically, back it with triangulated evidence, and offer a practical path through it.

---

## Confidence tagging (applied to every finding)

- **Very High** — triangulated across owner + 3+ team interviews + external signal / data
- **High** — triangulated across owner + 2+ team interviews OR owner + strong data signal
- **Medium** — owner hypothesis + partial validation, flagged for further probing

Never issue a recommendation without a confidence tag.
