Orientation

How skills work

Skills are specialised behaviours you invoke by typing /<skill-name> in Claude Code, or by hitting a trigger phrase. Say "we just signed X" and Claude will fire /jirah-new-client on its own. Say "daily briefing" and it will fire /jirah-daily-briefing.

Two classes of skill live in this workstation. Working skills are fully implemented and run immediately. Ghost skills are stubbed today. When invoked, a ghost skill returns the build brief you can see in this document. That brief is the spec. Once a ghost skill is promoted to working, its invocation starts delivering real output.

Jirah-specific skills live in .claude/skills/ inside this workspace and travel with it. Personal skills (html-brief, meeting-prep, decision-memo) live in Jason's global ~/.claude/skills/ and are available across every project.

Working skills · Available right now

The four Jirah skills you can run today

These are implemented end-to-end. Fire them and they execute. Everything below is the authoritative behaviour — if the skill diverges from this description, the SKILL.md file in .claude/skills/ is the source of truth.

/jirah-new-client
Working

Scaffolds a new Active Client folder from the OneDrive template

Copies 01 - Clients/_template/ into 01 - Clients/Active/[Client Name]/, preserving the full numbered scaffold (03 - Admin through 10 - Harvest). Fills 00 - Profile.md frontmatter from your inputs: id (slug), name, industry, geo, owner, co-owner, currentStep, milestone, contract, sponsor, status, created.

Seeds 02 - Comms Log.md with the engagement-opened entry. If an AI pilot is in scope, also creates the parallel ~\Desktop\Jirah-AI-[ClientName]\ workspace and cross-references it in the profile. Pairs with /jirah-file-sort: after scaffolding, point the sort skill at whatever documents the client sent.

Fire when"we just signed X" or "new client onboarding for Y". Asks for owner, industry, geo, contract value, sponsor before acting.
/jirah-file-sort
Working

Classifies and moves a batch of files into a client's numbered subfolders

Takes a source directory (Downloads, a handed-over folder, a list of paths) and a target client, then classifies each file using 22 filename and content heuristics: contracts, financials, legal, handbooks, marketing, discussion documents, questionnaires, interview guides, session recordings, stakeholder interviews, facilitator notes, drafts, finals, monthly meetings, AI pilot artefacts, harvest materials.

Shows a preview plan before moving anything — no silent moves. Optionally renames to YYYY-MM-DD - Title.ext. Uses robocopy under the hood to survive OneDrive sync locks. Ambiguous files land in _unsorted/ rather than getting guessed into the wrong place. Works for active clients, prospects, and audit applicants (prospects get a flatter scheme since they don't have the full 03–10 structure).

Fire when"sort these files into [Client]", "put these in the right place", "Genesis just sent us docs", or after /jirah-new-client on intake day.
/jirah-daily-briefing
Working

Scans the whole pipeline and drafts your morning briefing

Reads all active clients, prospects, and audit applications. Surfaces overdue follow-ups (based on lastContactDays), sprint-phase risks, warm prospects worth nurturing, and draft replies where the next action is communication. Writes briefings/YYYY-MM-DD.md for the day.

Designed to run on a scheduled trigger each morning, but you can also fire it manually any time you want to know what's on your plate.

Fire when"daily briefing", "what's on my plate", or on the scheduled morning trigger.
/jirah-friction-audit
Working

The 1-day paid audit product, end to end

Invoke with --mode prep, --mode capture, or --mode synthesize. The Friction Audit is Jirah's $1,500 paid front-door product: handpicked applicants book a full day, and the skill guides each phase — pre-read materials in prep, structured capture during the session, and triangulated synthesis after.

Diagnoses through the 10 Friction Points with confidence tags (Very High / High / Medium). Triangulation is non-negotiable. Produces a short action register with three expansion paths: ops, project, and AI. The AI path feeds directly into /jirah-ai-opportunity-scan when that ghost promotes to working.

Fire whena handpicked audit applicant has paid and booked their day. Prep 48 hours before. Capture during. Synthesize within 72 hours after.
Ghost skills · Build briefs

Thirty-one Jirah ghosts, organised by lifecycle phase

Each ghost below is a spec waiting to be promoted to a working skill. Invoking one today returns the build brief. The phase groupings mirror the Jirah engagement lifecycle: demand generation at top of funnel, intake and discovery, sprint and report, parallel AI track, retainer, harvest loop, and ops cadence on top.

Demand Generation

8 skills · top of funnel
/jirah-industry-pulse
Weekly scan across Jirah's four verticals (engineering, professional services, accounting, building systems) for outreach triggers: funding rounds, senior hires, partner changes, expansion announcements. Outputs a ranked target list with specific-observation hooks you can quote in cold outreach. Fire weekly, typically Monday morning.
/jirah-lead-gen
Deep research on a batch of target prospects, then drafts partner-voice intro emails that reference specific observations (not generic boilerplate). Fans out to the jirah-prospect-researcher subagent in parallel to keep the main context clean. Fire when you have 3–10 prospects to warm up and want drafts ready to send.
/jirah-intro-request
Drafts warm-intro asks off the people graph. Produces the email that an introducer can forward verbatim — so the friction for them is zero. Fire when an existing contact could bridge you to a named prospect.
/jirah-thought-leadership
LinkedIn posts and longform pieces in Jirah's signature "owner thought X, actual was Y" format. Weekly post cadence, monthly longform cadence. Pulls patterns from the pattern library so the content is grounded in real engagement learning, not generic advice.
/jirah-audit-apply
Generates the landing page copy, application form questions, and scoring rubric for the "Apply for a Friction Audit" funnel front door. Designed for out-of-market prospects who don't get the lunch invite but still deserve a path in. Fire once to stand up the page, then refresh as positioning evolves.
/jirah-audit-triage
ICP-scores incoming audit applications against the criteria (30–150 staff, $6M–$20M revenue, owner-run B2B services, the four core verticals). Ranks them, drafts accept and decline emails, and updates application status. Fire as applications arrive or in a weekly batch.
/jirah-people-graph
Tracks and queries the wider Jirah relationship network: introducers, alumni of past engagements, advisors, prospects' colleagues. One markdown file per person. Fire to find who could warm a cold prospect, or to see who Jirah knows inside a target firm.
/jirah-email
Partner-voice emails across every outreach stage: cold intro, follow-up, proposal send, mid-engagement check-in, warm nurture. Signs "Jason and Joshua" for joint emails, single partner for solo sends. Fire when you need a draft that sounds like Jirah rather than generic consultant-ese.

Discovery & Discussion

3 skills · Steps 1–2
/jirah-discovery
Step 1 of the 6-step process. Preps for a discovery call (questions, hypotheses to probe, context from prior touches) and produces a post-call synthesis with a binary fit decision and clear next step. Fire before and after every first qualifying call.
/jirah-discussion-document
Step 2 deliverable. Produces the Mode A Discussion Document that codifies objectives, scope, timeline, and investment. Pattern sourced from the Smith & Anderson exemplar. This is what converts a qualified discovery into a signed engagement. Fire after discovery when the prospect has signalled interest.
/jirah-survey-writing
Designs stakeholder surveys: friction-point-indexed questions with an NPS and open-ended mix. Used pre-audit to gather quantitative signal before the day, or mid-engagement for triangulation. Fire when you need a survey out the door and don't want to reinvent the structure.

Sprint & Report

8 skills · Steps 3–5
/jirah-kickoff-agenda
Produces the 60–90 minute Step 3 kickoff meeting agenda plus internal prep notes for the facilitator pair. Sets expectations, confirms scope, and locks the sprint dates. Fire 3–5 days before kickoff.
/jirah-sprint-facilitation
Step 4 support. Delivers the pre-sprint preparation guide and, during the sprint, an in-session transcript analyser that spawns jirah-transcript-analyzer subagents on each Fireflies transcript as sessions land. Fire once before the sprint and then on each session recording.
/jirah-session-analyzer
Processes a single Fireflies transcript via the transcript-analyzer subagent. Extracts language shifts, contradictions, and emotional markers. Called by /jirah-sprint-facilitation but also usable standalone. Fire on any transcript you want to mine for signal.
/jirah-team-interview
Interview guides for team-member sessions (triangulation during audits and engagements). Role-specific friction-point probing — the questions for a long-tenured IC differ from the ones for the ops lead. Fire when booking team interviews.
/jirah-triangulation
Utility skill. For a given owner hypothesis, plans the validation: who to interview, what external checks to run, what data to pull. Fire when the owner has just stated a strong claim and you need to decide how to stress-test it.
/jirah-financial-audit
Reviews client financials (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, AR aging) for red flags: margin compression, concentration risk, aging receivables, unusual expense patterns. Feeds the Friction Audit afternoon data pass. Fire when financials arrive and you need them read before the session.
/jirah-document-review
Reviews client-sent documents (strategy decks, policies, financials, legal) via the jirah-document-reviewer subagent. Surfaces concerns to raise with the owner — gaps, contradictions, red flags. Fire on any substantive document the client sends.
/jirah-action-register
Step 5 final deliverable. Produces the prioritised action register: Critical / High / Medium, mapped to friction point, owner-assigned, deadline-bound. This is the register, not a narrative report — that's the whole point. Replaces the retired /jirah-report-writing. Fire after sprint synthesis.
/jirah-report-review
Methodology compliance check on a drafted Jirah deliverable. Confidence tags on every finding, action-register format, voice match, Mode A visuals, no out-of-scope items. Extends the global /doc-review-jason with Jirah-specific criteria. Fire before any client-facing report goes out.
/jirah-client-presentation
Builds client-facing HTML presentations — extends the global /html-brief-jason with Jirah branding (Mode A standalone or Mode B co-branded). Single-file HTML, browser-rendered. Fire for Discussion Documents, final-report summaries, pilot plans when a deck-style presentation is required.

AI Track

4 skills · parallel to core consulting
/jirah-ai-opportunity-scan
Embedded in every Friction Audit. Surfaces where AI creates 10x leverage in the client's workflow. Outputs 2–3 pilot-shaped opportunities — concrete enough to scope, not so specific that the Plan A/B fork is foreclosed. Fire as part of every audit synthesis.
/jirah-ai-scoping
Turns one selected AI opportunity into a scoped 4-week pilot proposal. Mode B (co-branded), Plan A/B decision fork, weekly deliverables, 0-hallucination standards. Fire after the owner has picked the opportunity they want to pursue.
/jirah-pilot-plan
Produces the client-facing AI Pilot Plan document (Mode B co-branded). Structure: Hero → Problem → Approach → Timeline → Example Output → Risks → Next Steps. Single-file HTML via the pilot-plan template. Fire when the scope is signed and you need the deliverable-grade document.
/jirah-ai-pilot-status
Weekly demo-able status document during AI pilot execution. Shows progress, names risks, surfaces Plan A / Plan B decision state. Designed to be screenshared live with the client, not emailed as a dense report. Fire every Friday during an active pilot.

Ops Cadence

4 skills · partner rhythm
/jirah-weekly-review
Monday partner-sync synthesis. Pipeline movement, overdue follow-ups, this-week content slate, audits triaged this week, engagements at risk. Pairs with /jirah-daily-briefing — the daily is tactical, the weekly is strategic. Fire Monday morning.

Probabilistic pipeline forecast from the stage data. Applies conversion rates to weighted value. Outputs expected close this quarter with an uncertainty band — not a single number. Fire monthly or when the pipeline shape changes materially.

Scans prior engagements (Vulcan → Hatch → Falcon → Genesis and everything since) for patterns matching a current client profile. One-shot retrospective across all ~50 historical Jirah proposals. Fans out 5 jirah-proposal-miner subagents in parallel. Highest-ROI single build — validates the ICP with data, surfaces price-point patterns, and names revivable lost-deal candidates. Fire once now, then again each year to refresh.

On ghost skills

Why a skill can exist before it works

Ghost skills are stubs. Invoking one today returns a build brief: purpose, inputs, process outline, output shape. That brief is the spec. When the skill gets promoted to working, that spec becomes the implementation contract — so the behaviour is predictable from day one.

This pattern lets the full skill registry exist now, even though only four Jirah skills are implemented. The registry makes the intended behaviour legible, surfaces naming collisions early, and lets Claude trigger the right skill by phrase even when the body is stubbed (it will return the brief and you can either hand-execute the steps or schedule the build).

Priority order for promoting ghosts lives in PLAN.md §4 in this workspace.

Using skills well. Invoke by slash command when you want to be explicit (/jirah-daily-briefing). Use natural phrasing when you want Claude to pick the skill ("run my daily briefing"). Both work. If you're not sure whether a skill exists, ask — "is there a skill for X?" — and Claude will check the registry.

Adding a new skill. Drop a SKILL.md file under .claude/skills/<skill-name>/ with the frontmatter schema used by the existing skills. The runtime picks it up automatically on the next session.
